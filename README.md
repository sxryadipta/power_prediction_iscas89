**ISCAS'89 Benchmark Suite** — standard sequential circuit benchmarks.

| Split | Circuits | Features | Target |
|-------|----------|----------|--------|
| Train | 20       | 9        | Power (W) |
| Test  | 5        | 9        | Power (W) |

**Target:** Power consumption (Watts)

## ⭐ Novelty — SHAP Feature Importance

SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) is applied to both XGBoost and Random
Forest to identify which circuit structural features drive power consumption.

Key findings:
- **Total gate count** is the dominant predictor (SHAP >> all other features)
- Both tree models agree on feature ranking → model-independent finding
- This explains why tree-based models outperform SVR/KNN: they exploit
  the hierarchical, non-linear gate-power relationship

---

## 📈 Evaluation

Due to the small dataset (n=25), **Leave-One-Out Cross-Validation (LOOCV)**
is used as the primary evaluation strategy alongside a standard train/test split.
